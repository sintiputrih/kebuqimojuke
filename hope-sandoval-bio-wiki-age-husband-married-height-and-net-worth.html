Hope Sandoval and The Warm Inventions

Sandoval founded The Warm Inventions in 2000 and recorded her first solo album, Bavarian Fruit Bread. It is known that she recorded it with My Bloody Valentine drummer Colm Ó Cíosóig in 2001. However, the album differed from that one with Mazzy Star in terms of theme, voice, and instrumentation.

Nonetheless, with one video on MTV and little radio play, they didn’t win commercial success. Sandoval recorded a song called “Wild Roses” for a compilation CD released by Air France, In the Air 2008.

Furthermore, Hope and The Warm Inventions released their second album, Through the Devil Softly. Along with her band were chosen by Matt Groening to play at the All Tomorrow’s Parties festival.

At the request of film director Jim Jarmusch, the band also performed at the ATP New York 2010 music festival in Monticello, New York in September 2010.

Mazzy Star Hope Sandoval

Sandoval performed with the band Opal in the late 1980s with David Roback and Kendra Smith. However, she assumed over lead vocals after Smith’s sudden departure during a tour of the UK. It is known that she hurled her guitar to the floor at the Hammersmith gig.

Citing this, Roback and Sandoval started writing together at the end of the tour. Furthermore, the duo founded the alternative rock band Mazzy Star. Their very first Mazzy Star album, named, She Hangs Brightly, was released in 1990.

Most noteworthy, the album identified Mazzy Star as a band with a unique sound although not a business success. The band had released a surprise breakout hit song in October 1993.

Sandoval lead singer of Mazzy Star, In 2009, confirmed in an interview with Rolling Stone that Mazzy Star was still active. The group said they were planning to release the album in 2012. The first single from the new album was released in July 2013, “California.”

Hope Sandoval Pays Tribute to David Roback

Sandoval paid tribute to her Mazzy Star bandmate David Roback on March 4, 2020. Unfortunately, Roback died on February 25, 2020, at the age of 61 due to metastatic cancer. Their band, Mazzy Star, recorded a trio of well-received songs in the ’90s, with 1993’s So Tonight That I Might See becoming the most enduring.
