Andrea Evans was a famous American actress and producer who was most recognized for her portrayal of Tina Lord on the ABC soap opera One Life to Live, a role she played from 1978 to 1981 and from 1985 to 1990, returning in 2008 and 2011.

Evans began her acting career in the late 1970s, making appearances in popular soap operas such as “The Young and the Restless” and “Days of Our Lives.”

However, it was her role as Tina Lord on the long-running soap opera “One Life to Live” that catapulted her to stardom.

Portraying the complex character of Tina from 1978 to 1981 and then again from 1985 to 1990, Evans captivated audiences with her exceptional acting skills and undeniable screen presence.

During her time on “One Life to Live,” Evans received critical acclaim for her portrayal of Tina Lord.

She earned several Daytime Emmy Award nominations for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series and won the award in 1990.

Her character’s tumultuous love story with Cord Roberts (played by John Loprieno) became one of the most memorable storylines in soap opera history.

After leaving “One Life to Live,” Evans continued to showcase her versatility as an actress by taking on various roles in both television and film. She appeared in shows like “Pacific Palisades,” “Passions,” and “The Bold and the Beautiful.” Additionally, she starred in movies such as “The Arrangement” and “Storm War.”

Evans also made a triumphant return to daytime television when she reprised her role as Tina Lord on “One Life to Live” for a brief period between 2008 and 2011.

Her comeback delighted fans who had missed seeing her captivating performances on their screens.
