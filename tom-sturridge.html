Tom Sturridge (born on December 21, 1985; Age:36) is a British actor from Lambeth, London. He has been working in the acting world since 1996. His first project, “Gulliver’s Travels,” came when he was only eleven. The following year he was in a show called “FairyTale: A True Story.”

Tom has a prominent place in the British TV industry. He has worked on some of the most popular English shows like “Sweetbitter” and “Velvet Buzzsaw.” He also appeared alongside actors like Benedict Cumberbatch and Tom Hiddleston in the 2012 series “The Hollow Crown.”

In 2022, Sturridge became a topic of talk after he landed the lead role, Dream in Neil Gaiman’s horror-fantasy drama “Sandman,” which aired on August 5 on Netflix. This star-studded show has actors like Boyd Holbrook, Jenna Coleman, Gwendoline Christie, Mason Alexander Park, and Kirby Howell-Baptiste.

Born & Raised in the UK

Thomas Sidney Jerome Sturridge (36 years old) was born on December 21, 1985, in Lambeth, London, to BAFTA-winning father Charles Sturridge and TV star mother Phoebe Nicholls. He is the family’s eldest child, with a younger brother called Arthur and a sister named Matilda.

Due to his parents being associated with the acting world, Tom had decided in his childhood that he would also become an actor when he grew up. He was only 11 years old when he started his career with a show. Like Tommy, his sister Matilda debuted when she was just a kid.

Besides acting, Tom also paid full attention to his studies and attended The Harrodian School till 1999. After Harrodian, he joined Winchester College and studied there until 2001.

How did Tom Sturridge’s Acting Career Start?

After earning the spotlight in 1997, Tom kept getting more and more roles. But his parents wanted him to complete his schooling first, so he stayed away from the camera between 1997 and 2003. In 2004 Tommy returned to the screen as Young Georgy and Roger Gosselin in “Vanity Fair” and “Being Julia,” respectively.

From 2005 to 2010, he worked in blockbuster movies like “Brothers of the Head,” “Murderous Intent,” “The Boat That Rocked” and “Waiting for Forever.” Many people do not know that the makers of “Jumper” had chosen Tom to play the lead role in the movie. Later, production houses decided they did not want to spend $100 million on a project with an unknown actor, so they replaced Sturridge with Hayden Christensen.
