Career and Professional Life

Francis Suarez is the city of Miami’s 33rd mayor. As the city’s first native-born mayor, Suarez pledges to work tirelessly to assist the citizens of the neighborhood where he grew up. Mayor Suarez served as Miami Commissioner for District 4 for eight years before being elected with 86 percent support from Miami people.

As Commissioner, Miami Mayor Suarez accomplished a great deal. It entails putting ShotSpotter technology in the city — a cutting-edge gunshot detection system – as well as establishing a transportation trust fund to fund existing and future transit developments.

His other accomplishments include the passage of the Reverse Redline Act, which allows for litigation against many large banks for discriminatory mortgage lending practices. As a result, the City’s complaint was heard by the United States Supreme Court, which found that localities have the standing to challenge banks for such discriminatory acts under the Fair Housing Act.

Suarez served as a member of the Miami City Commission from the 4th District from November 2009 to the same month in 2017. He took over for Tomás Regalado, while Manolo Reyes took over for Suarez. Furthermore, on November 15, 2017, Suarez was sworn in as Mayor of Miami.
