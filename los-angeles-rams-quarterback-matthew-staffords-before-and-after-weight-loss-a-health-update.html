Los Angeles Rams quarterback Matthew Stafford’s weight loss: A health update

After gaining weight early in his career, L.A. Rams quarterback Matthew Stafford utilized yoga and swimming to keep in shape. And that contributed to his Super Bowl victory.

In order to remain healthy and play the game longer, Stafford claimed he is attempting to do so. He freely admitted that his fitness and health are factors in his metamorphosis due to weight reduction.

He has shed weight while working out, and he was asked on a SiriusXM NFL Radio broadcast from camp about what he had changed to bring about such alluring changes.

“Same old, same old,” said Stafford with a smirk. I’m simply trying to play the game and keep myself as healthy as I can. All throughout the year, it happens.

In addition, when asked for a more thorough explanation, he said that it’s so he can get along with teammates on the field.

Similar to how Stafford purportedly showed up to training camp in 2018 at his leanest and slimmest point in his NFL career.

He said in an interview that he had never been more dedicated to his diet and workout.

Interestingly, Stafford’s frequent swimming provided him with a cardiovascular exercise that strengthened every muscle in his body, which is a better way to avoid joint issues than hard weightlifting.

Stafford Attributed Winning the Super Bowl to His Weight Loss

After years of coming up short, Stafford attributed his new focus on fitness and commitment to weight reduction as one of the key factors in his ultimate Super Bowl victory.

“There are probably some old images floating around of him, at 235 lbs and enjoying life back in the day, playing ball at a more significant weight,” Stafford told Insider during a Super Bowl news conference.

But as he grows older, he realizes how important fitness is. He has always made an effort to be in excellent physical shape and be ready to play.

In an interview with A Healthier Michigan.org, Stafford, who is now listed at 220 pounds, said that regular swimming and yoga, along with dog walks to improve his flexibility and cardio, are two of the healthiest habits he has developed over the last several years.
