King Charles Attending 2024 Easter Service Amid Cancer

 Posted on April 24, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  371 words  |  Larita Shotwell

King Charles will be front and center for Easter festivities and church service this year, BBC reports.

The monarch, alongside Queen Camilla, will be present at St. George's Chapel at Windsor Castle on Easter Sunday, Buckingham Palace confirmed. Charles' presence at the event is a major one, as it marks one of his most significant events he's attended since revealing that he was being treated for an undisclosed form of cancer. His presence, per the outlet, is being taking as "an encouraging sign about his health."

Senior royal members of the royal family are also expected to be at the event, however, Prince William, Kate Middleton and their three children, Prince George, Princess Charlotte and Prince Louis, will not be present at the church service.

William and Kate taking a step back from the event — an important one in the royal calendar — comes amid her revealing on March 22 that she had been diagnosed with an undisclosed form of cancer while she was recovering from abdominal surgery.

"In January, I underwent major abdominal surgery in London, and at the time, it was thought that my condition was non-cancerous," she explained in her announcement video. "The surgery was successful. However, tests after the operation found cancer had been present. My medical team, therefore, advised that I should undergo a course of preventative chemotherapy, and I’m now in the early stages of that treatment."

Middleton added that she hopes the public will respect her privacy as she received treatment, and plans to make a return when she's better. "We hope that you’ll understand that as a family, we now need some time, space, and privacy while I complete my treatment," she said. "My work has always brought me a deep sense of joy, and I look forward to being back when I am able. But for now, I must focus on making a full recovery."

Prior to her announcement, she was expected to return to her royal duties after Easter, which falls on March 31. "The Princess will return to official duties when she is cleared to do so by her medical team," a palace representative said. "She is in good spirits and is focused on making a full recovery."
