Rags to Riches: The Incredible Net Worth of Lorraine Bracco
Written By: Author

Lorraine Bracco’s incredible career has led to an estimated net worth of a staggering $19 million. Her journey began on the 2nd of October in 1954, in Bay Ridge, Brooklyn, New York City, where she was born to a mixed heritage of French, English, Italian, and American descent. Bracco’s acting prowess has earned her accolades such as Academy Award, Golden Globe, and Emmy nominations, as well as a Screen Actors Guild Award win. She is widely recognized for her exceptional performances in the critically acclaimed biographical crime film “Goodfellas” (1990) and the HBO crime drama series “The Sopranos” (1999–2007). Bracco has been an influential figure in the entertainment industry since 1979.

Lorraine Bracco’s wealth is estimated at $19 million, as of 2016, with her earnings stemming from various sources, including her line of wines, “Bracco Wines”.

Bracco’s upbringing in Westbury, Long Island, following her matriculation from Hicksville High School in 1972, led her on a remarkable journey. In the early 1970s, she ventured to France where her career took off as a model for Jean-Paul Gaultier, and where she also crossed paths with the director and writer Lina Wertmüller. Her talents were noticed and she was chosen for a role in Wertmüller’s Italian film “Camorra”.

The pinnacle of her career came when she landed the supporting role of Karen Hill in “Goodfellas” (1990), directed by Martin Scorsese. Her portrayal earned her nominations for Academy and Golden Globe Awards, as well as accolades such as the Chicago Film Critics Association Award, Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award, and New York Film Critics Circle Awards for Best Supporting Actress. Bracco’s contributions to other films, including “Medicine Man” (1992), “Even Cowgirls Get the Blues” (1993), and “The Basketball Diaries” (1995), further added to her impressive net worth.

On the television front, Bracco played a pivotal role in the acclaimed series “The Sopranos” (1999-2007). Her remarkable performance earned her several nominations, including Screen Actors Guild, Golden Globe, Satellite, and Emmy Awards. She continued to grace the small screen with appearances in popular television series such as “Law & Order: Trial by Jury” (2005), “Lipstick Jungle” (2008), and “Law & Order: Criminal Intent” (2010). She has been a prominent figure in the television series “Rizzoli & Isles” since 2010. Furthermore, it was announced at the end of 2015 that she would star alongside Adrien Brody in the comedy series “Dice”.

In her personal life, Bracco has been married and divorced twice. Her first marriage was with Daniel Guerard, and she later shared a relationship with actor Harvey Keitel. After parting ways with Keitel, she married Edward James Olmos. She is a proud mother to two children, Stella Keitel and Margaux Guerard.

Lorraine Bracco’s incredible journey to success has placed her among the most esteemed personalities in the entertainment industry. Her talent, dedication, and versatility continue to be an inspiration to many.
