What Happened to Former NFL Star Kevin Kolb?

 Posted on August 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  441 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Former NFL star Kevin Kolb, once a promising quarterback, faced a series of challenges and setbacks that impacted his career. Let's delve into the details to understand what happened to him.

Early Career and College Success

Kevin Benjamin Kolb was born on August 24, 1984, in Victoria, Texas. He attended Stephenville High School in Texas, where he excelled as a three-year starter at quarterback before moving on to the University of Houston. His performance at Houston earned him the Conference USA Offensive MVP honors in 2006.

NFL Career with the Philadelphia Eagles

In the 2007 NFL Draft, Kolb was selected by the Philadelphia Eagles in the 2nd round as the 36th overall pick. He started as a backup to Donovan McNabb. However, Kolb got his chance to shine during the 2009 NFL season when he filled in for the injured McNabb. He made history by becoming the first quarterback in NFL history to throw for more than 300 yards in each of his first two career starts. Despite expressing his desire to be the starting quarterback for the Eagles during the 2010 offseason, his journey with the Eagles was marked by both promise and adversity.

Transition to the Arizona Cardinals

Following McNabb's trade to the Washington Redskins in April 2010, Kolb became the starting quarterback for the Eagles. However, after suffering a concussion in week 1, he was replaced by Michael Vick. Subsequently, Kolb was traded to the Arizona Cardinals prior to the start of the 2011 season. He signed a new five-year contract worth $63.5 million with the Cardinals. Kolb's tenure with the Cardinals was marked by both impressive performances and injury setbacks.

Struggles and Injuries

During his time with the Cardinals, Kolb faced challenges with injuries, including a concussion-related symptoms that forced him to miss several games. His performance was also affected by injuries, impacting his ability to secure a consistent starting position.

Retirement from Professional Football

Kolb was released by the Cardinals on March 15, 2013, marking the end of his professional football career. His retirement from the NFL marked the conclusion of a journey that was filled with both accomplishments and the adversity of injuries.

Legacy and Life After Football

Despite the challenges he faced during his NFL career, Kolb's legacy as a talented quarterback and his contributions to the teams he played for remain noteworthy. Following his retirement, he has ventured into other endeavors beyond football.

Kevin Kolb's NFL career, though marked by promising moments and setbacks, ultimately led to his retirement from professional football. His journey serves as a reminder of the unpredictable nature of sports and the resilience required to navigate its challenges.
