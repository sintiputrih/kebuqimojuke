Here’s your final cookie! These Cream Cheese Pecan Jam Thumbprint Cookies will make you want to keep eating them! They aren’t overly sweet and have such a nice crunch to them. It is a recipe that I have been tweaking for the past 10 years and something I make every Christmas for my kids. They get so excited to see mama’s jam cookies coming out of the oven. I used to make jam thumbprint cookies where you baked jam inside a buttery shortbread cookie. Even though they are so delicious and popular during the holidays, I wanted to experiment doing something different and add the jam AFTER the cookies came out of the oven.

These Cream Cheese Pecan Jam Thumbprint Cookies are made with softened cream cheese and butter, whipped until nice and creamy. A mixture of both brown sugar and sugar are added and creamed for several minutes. An egg and a generous amount of vanilla extract are added. Flour, baking soda, salt, and pecans are mixed in.  These Cream Cheese Pecan Jam Thumbprints are baked until golden brown and then topped with fresh jam.

  • Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

  • In a large mixing bowl, cream softened butter and cream cheese for 2 minutes. Add brown sugar and sugar and cream for 4 minutes. Add egg and vanilla and mix together, scraping the bowl.

  • Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt. Add pecans.

  • Chill for 10 minutes.

  • Drop cookie dough onto parchment or Silpat lined baking sheet.

  • Bake for 10-12 minutes or until lightly golden brown on edges.

  • Remove from oven and sprinkle with sugar.

  • Lightly place thumb in center of cookie lightly pressing down to make an indention.

  • Drop a heaping 1 teaspoon of jam in the center of the cookie.
