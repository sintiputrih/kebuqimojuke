The best smart padlocks for 2023

 Posted on October 8, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1376 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Tired of relying on a pesky traditional deadbolt lock with a required key or numeric code? It sounds like you should consider upgrading to a smart padlock instead. Utilizing the same basic design as your standard deadbolt lock, a smart padlock adds features like random PIN generation, Bluetooth pairing capabilities, and even companion app controls.

If it's time to upgrade your padlocks to shield your smart home, we've rounded up some of the best smart padlocks below. Take a look at our guide, check out the features, and see what's right for you. You can also peruse our list of the best smart locks, if you want something for the front door.

MYPIN Smart Fingerprint Padlock

The best Bluetooth padlock

  • Integrates with your phone
  • Stores up to 10 fingerprints
  • IP65-rated
  • Fingerprint scanner can be a bit unresponsive
  • Only uses Bluetooth (no Wi-Fi connectivity)

The Mypin Smart Fingerprint Padlock is our favorite lock overall, and for a number of reasons. First and foremost, it comes loaded with all of the features we would expect to see on padlocks that are double, if not triple, the price. This includes an intelligent fingerprint sensor that stores up to 10 sets of prints, Bluetooth functionality for remotely unlocking it with a mobile device, and an IP65-rated body for water resistance and a rust-proof shackle.

While some users have complained that the fingerprint sensor can get a bit finicky when fingers are wet and shriveled from sweat or a swim, once hands are dry, the lock works just fine. While it may not be the most tech-heavy lock in our roundup, we believe that most folks will be completely satisfied with the capabilities and rugged design of the Mypin Smart Fingerprint Padlock.

For those looking for a low-profile smart lock for their luggage, the Boread Fingerprint Padlock measures a little over half an inch thick. Frequently Asked Questions

Are smart padlocks safe to use?

Absolutely. In fact, when you consider the range of additional security features you'll get with a smart padlock versus a traditional model, including things like fingerprint scanning, random PIN generation, and dashboard companion apps for monitoring lock activity, it becomes apparent just how much more safety your property and valuables receive when compared to the typical lock-and-key scenario.

Are smart padlocks expensive?

This all depends on what features you're looking for. If you're the kind of person that wants your tech outfitted with as many controls, customizations, and add-ons as possible, a fully-loaded smart padlock with an app, Bluetooth settings, and fingerprint scanning may cost upwards of $100, compared to a more basic smart padlock that may only pair to your phone using Bluetooth for remote controls.

That said, the average price for a smart padlock tends to be around $70.

Can I use a smart padlock without Wi-Fi?

Yes, you can, but you'll want to make sure you purchase a model that features both Bluetooth and Wi-Fi controls, or a smart padlock that only uses Bluetooth.

These days, a smart padlock with Wi-Fi capabilities is a big ask, with many models offering Bluetooth as the one and only means of remote controls. And while this may be inconvenient when it comes to monitoring your smart padlock from miles away, it ensures that your padlock will still be accessible in the event of a network outage.
